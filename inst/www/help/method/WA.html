Welch's ANOVA is used when 3 or more conditions are compared and the independent variable is a between-groups factor (different participants are used for different conditions). It is an alternative to the one-way ANOVA. It can be used used when homogeneity of variances is not met (i.e., the distributions have unequal variances). It still requires the distributions to follow normal distributions.

Conditions:

  • Within-subjects experimental design
  • Variables under examination should be measured in interval or ratio scale
  • Variables should have a linear relationship between the independent and dependent variable
  • Variables should not have significant outliners
  • Needs for dependent data to be normally distributed or homogeneity of variances
